CHANDIGARH India's first planned city, is a union territory serving as the shared capital of Punjab and Haryana. Known as "The City of Beauty", it's located near the foothills of the Himalayas. The city is internationally recognized for its architecture and urban design, largely influenced by Swiss-French architect Le Corbusier. It's part of the Chandigarh Capital Region, which includes the nearby cities of Panchkula and Mohali. With one of the highest per capita incomes in India, Chandigarh is a symbol of modern India's progress.

Places To Visit In Chandigarh

Rock Garden

The Rock Garden of Chandigarh is a unique 40-acre sculpture garden built by Nek Chand Saini from recycled industrial and home waste, attracting over 1.5 million visitors annually.

Sukhna Lake

Sukhna Lake, a serene 3 km² reservoir in Chandigarh, India, was created in 1958. It’s a popular spot for boating, birdwatching, and enjoying the tranquil surroundings.

Rose Garden

The Zakir Hussain Rose Garden in Chandigarh, India, is Asia’s largest rose garden. It spans 30 acres, housing 50,000 rose bushes of 1600 different species.

Govt Museum and Art Gallery

The Government Museum and Art Gallery in Chandigarh, designed by Le Corbusier, houses a rich collection of Gandharan sculptures, Pahari and Rajasthani miniature paintings.
